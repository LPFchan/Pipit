## Product Thesis

Pipit is the mobile companion app for the Immogen immobilizer ecosystem. It gives owners and guests a phone-based key that can unlock or lock a compatible vehicle over BLE, manage provisioned slots securely, and recover or migrate keys without requiring a cloud service.

## Primary User And Context

- Primary operator: An owner or trusted guest using a phone as a vehicle key.
- Primary environment: A Ninebot Max G30 or similar compatible vehicle fitted with the Immogen immobilizer hardware, plus a phone running iOS or Android.
- Primary problem being solved: Replace or complement the dedicated Uguisu fob with a secure phone workflow for proximity unlock, manual lock or unlock, provisioning, migration, and recovery.
- Why this matters: The system needs to stay usable offline, preserve strict anti-replay guarantees, and keep administration tied to slot-based access control rather than cloud identity.

## Primary Workspace Object

The primary user-facing object is a provisioned phone key bound to one of Pipit's four firmware slot identities and backed by a locally stored AES key plus monotonic counter state.

## Canonical Interaction Model

1. A user provisions or recovers a phone key through a QR flow generated by Whimbrel or through a recovery path opened by the vehicle and firmware.
2. Pipit stores the imported slot key and counter state locally using platform secure storage.
3. In the background, Pipit watches for the vehicle's BLE advertisements and evaluates proximity thresholds to trigger lock or unlock behavior.
4. In the foreground, the user can manually lock or unlock from the fob-style home surface.
5. Onboarding and Settings use the BLE management transport to inspect slots, rename or revoke keys, provision guests, and perform recovery or migration flows.
6. The firmware and shared crypto layers enforce slot routing, strict monotonic counters, and authenticated management operations.

## Core Capabilities

- Capability: Background proximity unlock and lock.
  - Why it exists: The app must behave like a passive key when the user approaches or leaves the vehicle.
  - What must remain true: Thresholding, hysteresis, and security rules are enforced through the shared settings and BLE transport rather than ad hoc UI logic.
- Capability: Manual foreground fob interaction.
  - Why it exists: Users need an explicit lock or unlock surface even when background automation is disabled or unavailable.
  - What must remain true: Manual commands use the same shared slot keys and counter discipline as background actions.
- Capability: Multi-slot key management.
  - Why it exists: Owners need to provision, rename, revoke, migrate, and recover owner and guest keys.
  - What must remain true: Slot semantics remain fixed by internal slot ID and management privileges are not relaxed.
- Capability: Offline provisioning and recovery.
  - Why it exists: Users must be able to bring up or recover the system without a cloud dependency.
  - What must remain true: QR flows interoperate with Whimbrel and preserve the existing PIN, Argon2id, and AES-CCM model.

## Invariants

- Slot identities are fixed: internal slot `0` is Uguisu hardware, `1` is owner, and `2` to `3` are guest slots.
- Monotonic counters remain strict with no replay window.
- Pipit remains a native mobile app with platform-specific BLE, secure storage, camera, and background-service integrations.
- Protocol, provisioning, and security behavior must stay compatible with the Immogen firmware and Whimbrel QR flow.
- Project-level truth lives in `SPEC.md`, `STATUS.md`, and `PLANS.md`; deep protocol and system details live in `PIPIT_MASTER_ARCHITECTURE.md`.

## Non-Goals

- A cloud-backed account system for key ownership or synchronization.
- A single cross-platform UI abstraction that hides platform-specific BLE or OS behavior.
- Relaxing slot permissions, pairing rules, or counter requirements for convenience.

## Main Surfaces

- Surface: Onboarding and recovery
  - Purpose: Import owner or guest keys, decrypt encrypted owner transfers, and recover keys when the provisioning window is opened.
  - Notes: Tied to QR parsing, shared Argon2id support, secure storage, and BLE management flows.
- Surface: Home fob
  - Purpose: Present the primary manual lock or unlock control and foreground connection state.
  - Notes: Uses a 3D fob presentation backed by platform-native UI plus bundled viewer or asset pipelines.
- Surface: Settings and key management
  - Purpose: Inspect slots, tune proximity thresholds, manage owner or guest keys, and invoke USB-assisted tasks where applicable.
  - Notes: Depends on the shared BLE management transport and Android USB backend rather than duplicating transport logic in UI code.

## Success Criteria

- Owners can provision and recover a phone key that interoperates with the current Immogen firmware and Whimbrel dashboard.
- Both mobile platforms can perform secure manual and proximity-based lock or unlock operations using the shared crypto model.
- Key management flows remain local-first, slot-aware, and testable without introducing cloud coupling or security regressions.
